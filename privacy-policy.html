3. Information We Collect

A. Information You Provide Directly

When you contact us or receive services, you may provide:

  • Name
  • Contact information (email, phone number, mailing address)
  • Date of birth and demographic information
  • Insurance details, if applicable
  • Health-related information relevant to your care (in clinical settings only)
  • Information shared through phone, secure messaging, or during visits

B. Website & Usage Data

When you visit our website, we may collect limited technical information such as:

  • IP address
  • Device and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent on the site

This information is used only to improve the website experience and is not used as part of your medical record.

4. Protected Health Information (PHI) & HIPAA

Any health information that identifies you and is created or received as part of your treatment is considered Protected Health Information (PHI). We are required by law to:

  • Maintain the privacy and security of your PHI
  • Provide you with a Notice of Privacy Practices upon establishing care
  • Use and disclose PHI only as permitted or required by law

We do not disclose your PHI to third parties for marketing or sales purposes. PHI is shared only for treatment, payment, healthcare operations, or when legally required (for example, safety concerns or court orders).

5. How We Use Your Information

We may use your information to:

  • Schedule and manage appointments
  • Provide psychiatric evaluation, diagnosis, and treatment
  • Coordinate care with your consent (e.g., with therapists, primary care providers)
  • Verify insurance coverage and process payments
  • Send appointment reminders and administrative messages
  • Respond to inquiries you send via phone, email, or web forms
  • Improve our services and website

6. SMS/Text Message Terms & Conditions

By providing your mobile phone number to Healing Minds, you may consent to receive SMS/text messages from us for the purposes described below. These messages are primarily administrative and are not used for marketing.

Frequency varies based on appointment activity but typically includes reminders prior to appointments and occasional follow-ups.

C. How to Opt Out

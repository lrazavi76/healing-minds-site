Grief & Life Transitions

Grief is a natural and deeply human response to loss — whether it’s the passing of a loved one, the end of a relationship, a major life change, or a shift in identity or purpose. Life transitions, even positive ones, can bring emotional challenges, uncertainty, and stress. You do not have to navigate these experiences alone.

Grief can affect mood, sleep, concentration, appetite, and overall well-being. It may come in waves, feel unpredictable, or bring up emotions you weren’t expecting. This is all a normal part of the healing process.

Support during grief and life transitions focuses on understanding your emotional experience, validating your feelings, and helping you regain stability and strength. When appropriate, medication may be used to ease symptoms such as anxiety, insomnia, or depression that may arise during these periods.

My goal is to provide a safe space where you can express your feelings openly, process your experience, and move forward at a pace that feels right for you. Healing doesn’t mean forgetting — it means learning to carry your experiences with clarity, resilience, and compassion toward yourself.
